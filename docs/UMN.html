U. Minnesota EEB Head

In Feb. 2020 I applied for a position as department head for the University of Minnesota Ecology, Evolution, and Behavior Department. I have made it to the interview stage in Sept. 2020. Here is information and materials relevant to my application. You may be interested in my CV and people who have been through my lab.

Science talk

Papers from talk

  • O’Meara, Smith, et al. 2016: paper on floral evolution. Note that it has two co-lead authors and two co-senior authors (Fenster and Diggle)

Leadership talk

General links from talk

  • Safe Evolution, especially the poster, and transparency reports for 2017-2018 and 2019
  • Academic Misconduct Database: Created initially by Julie Libarkin, tracks publicly resolved and ongoing cases of sexual misconduct perpetrated by U.S. faculty, administrators and other staff. See here for Minnesota cases.
  • Better Letter: Resource I made to make it easier to look for biases in letters of rec
  • Teaching: My senior level macroevolution class and graduate course in phylogenetics
  • UTK EEB Community of Practice: how we are ensuring labs can open safely while respecting the needs of everyone inside them
  • NSF Survey of Earned Doctorates: PhDs by various categories per year
  • NSF Awards Advanced Search: to look up awarded funding
  • scholar R package: for pulling publication information from Google Scholar

UMN-specific links

  • “Racism with a smile”: New York Times article on racism in Minneapolis following the attention garnered by George Floyd’s murder
  • Bias Reponse Referral Network: reporting incidents of bias, includes transparency reports of how past incidents were handled
  • Aurora Center for Advocacy & Education: help all victims/survivors/concerned people of sexual assault, relationship violence, stalking, and sexual harassment, including information on reporting options
  • Ureport: to report misconduct in general. Includes an anonymous reporting option. Also see https://onestop.umn.edu/terms-and-conditions/student-complaints-and-grievances for additional options open to students (and others, in some cases).
  • StarTribune article on grad students confronting racism, including the actions of UMN EEB students and faculty response
  • Student Ombuds: confidential resource for students to help resolve any university-related conflict or concern
  • Crisis services: includes crisis line, 612-301-4673
  • Self-Help: anonymous mental health screenings, alcohol assessment, and more
  • Student Counseling: Individual and group therapy and more
  • BIPOC Mental Health Collective: meets second Thursday of each month
  • Employee Assistance Program: confidential consulting on mental health, relationships, stress, and more
