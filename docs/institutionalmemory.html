Institutional Memory

I have been in this area for over a dozen years, so I have seen some changes through time. For new folks coming in it can be hard to understand the history, but history is important in knowing what has been tried, what people are responding to, and just in understanding this community. I typically use wayback machine links so people can see the documents as they were.

Diversity, equity, and inclusion

  • 2022: “Divisive concepts” law banning required training specified “divisive concepts” or requiring people to believe or affirm these concepts. Description of the law’s effects here. The UTK Provost’s Office has distributed information on the law’s effects.
  • 2016: UTK disbands its Office for Diversity following defunding from the state. This was prompted by a post from the office arguing for inclusive celebrations (rather than have a government organization celebrate Christmas or other religious holidays) that some objected to as well as a post that mentioned that not everyone uses gendered pronouns (again resulting in pushback). In both cases the university quickly took down the posts.
  • 2016: The Pride Center has been vandalized multiple times.
  • 2022: UTK was ranked most LGBTQIA+ unfriendly of 488 universities by the Princeton Review. This is based on asking students how much they agree with the statement that “students treat all persons equally, regardless of their sexual orientation and gender identity/expression”. UTK suggests a better indicator is the Campus Pride Index, which is based on survey responses provided by the institituion about institutional investment and resources.

Sexual misconduct

2018: Multiple reports (see overview here). The only named individual is Dr. Henri Grissino-Mayr, a geography professor. 2011-present: UT’s Title IX office publishes reports and other documents: https://titleix.utk.edu/about-the-office-of-title-ix/documents-archive/. This includes the number and kind of reports, alleged incidents, support measures, and resolutions.

Speech on campus

  • 2013-present: Speech and activities during Sex Week have generated great controversy (reviewed here). The Tennessee House voted 69-17 to condemn the organizers of Sex Week in 2014.
  • 2016: A UTK law professor called for running down people protesting a police shooting who were blocking a highway. After an investigation, this was assessed as protected speech with no disciplinary action required. In 2022 the professor was appointed to the Board of Fellows of the new Institute of American Civics at UTK. Bills have been proposed at least twice (2017 and 2021) giving drivers immunity for hitting protestors blocking roads.

Policing

There are many levels of police here: UTK has its own police force, the city of Knoxville has its own force, Knox County has its own force, and there are also state police (and various federal agencies). One thing that seemed unusual to me coming from Boston was that the county basically operates like a city: both the county and city have their own mayors, they set rules for zoning in their respective areas, etc.

  • 2022: Nearly all Black Knoxville (city) police say they face discrimination (article includes links to other recent stories on potential instances): https://web.archive.org/web/20221021025248/https://www.knoxnews.com/story/news/local/2022/10/21/nearly-all-black-knoxville-police-officers-say-they-face-discrimination/69577490007/
  • 2014: “Courtesy notifications” letting UT athletics know in advance if there are any criminal allegations against players. This practice is reported as no longer continuing, partly due to publicity from this case. https://web.archive.org/web/20220125094112/https://www.knoxnews.com/story/news/crime/2018/07/26/aj-johnson-rape-trial-police-ut-vols/839564002/

Conservation

Snail darter

  • TVA was building the Tellico Dam, with the project starting in 1966.
  • In 1973, UTK faculty member David Etnier discovered a new species where the dam was being built by doing a survey with students of the area that would be affected. He described it (formally naming it according to the rules of species taxonomy), worked with others to get it listed under the shiny new Endangered Species Act, and publicly advocated for its salvation.
  • UTK law student Hank Hill, UT law prof Zyg Plater, and college of law assistant dean Don Cohen filed a federal suit to stop the dam.
  • The suit went up to the US Supreme Court – where Hill et al. won, stopping the dam.
  • Senator Baker (Baker Center namesake) and Senator Culver (Iowa) sponsored an Endangered Species Act amendment creating the “God Squad” or “God Committee” – a committee that can choose to allow the extinction of a species by exempting a federal agency (perhaps not coincidentally, such as TVA) from Endangered Species Act requirements.
  • The amendment passed; the committee met; the committee rejected TVA’s arguments largely on economic grounds, leaving the dam project stopped.
  • Senator Baker added a rider to a 1980 appropriations bill that exempted Tellico Dam from the Endangered Species Act. It passed.
  • The dam was completed, the lake began to fill, and 20 species (according to the TVA) were eliminated from the impounded river….
  • … but not from the planet (yet). Among them was the snail darter, which had been transplanted to a nearby river (and later, other populations were found). And now, the darter has recovered enough to be taken off the endangered species list.

Sports

Major sports here are men’s football, women’s basketball, and men’s basketball (men’s baseball is recently very popular, too). The teams compete in the SouthEastern Conference (SEC). They are generally called the Vols or Volunteers. There is tremendous fan support for the teams.

Men’s Football

The football team’s record is available here – note especially the orange and yellow rows when they won the national and conference championships, respectively. If they win half their games, they generally get to go to a bowl game. The quality of opponents can vary (some opponents are paid to play and the UTK team is expected to win many of those games) – the record against conference opponents is considered a better assessment of how the team is doing that year (and the SEC is one of the most difficult conferences). The Vols were one of the powerhouse teams for many years, then declined, but there is hope again this year (Fall 2022). The football coach is traditionally the highest paid employee of all state employees. Football coaches generally sign on for multiyear contracts: if fired they generally keep being paid (unless fired for cause, as happened in 2021), and if someone else tries to hire them away there generally is money paid to UTK in compensation (making hiring them away more expensive for competitors). UTK has a history of paying fired coaches or other personnel, though I am unaware of a comparison of UTK’s payouts with those of other schools.
